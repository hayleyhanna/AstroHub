Earth's Closest Nebula

Earth's closest known nebula is called the Helix Nebula. The Helix Nebula is approximately 700 light-years away. It is often called the "Eye of God" nebula due to its striking resemblance to an eye.

What is an Exoplanet?

An exoplanet or an extrasolar planet is a planet detected outside of our solar system. To date there are more than 4,000 exoplanets that are considered to be "confirmed". Astronomers calculate that for every star in our galaxy there is roughly one exoplanet.

The Point of No Return

The event horizon is the boundary that marks the limits of a black hole.At the event horizon the escape velocity is equal to the speed of light.General relativity states that nothing can travel faster than light,meaning nothing inside the event horizon will ever be able to escape beyond it,including light.

Enormous Water Reservoir

In 2011 astronomers found an enormous water vapor cloud about 12 billion light-years from Earth.The cloud is the oldest and largest source of water
in the universe that we know of.The water inside is equivalent to 140 trillion times the amount of water found in the Earths oceans.

The Suns Massive Size

The Sun's surface area is 11,900 times that of Earth's.It holds 99.8% of the solar system's mass and is roughly 109 times the diameter of Earth. The Sun is so large that if you squished as many Earth's inside of it as you could without any wasted space you would be able to fit around 1,300,000 Earths.
